# Excel-Portfolio
Country Sales Dashboard and Product Category Analysis
This description analyzes a Country Sales Performance Dashboard retrospective for the period 2011 to 2016. This era was a transformative time for the cycling industry, marked by the recovery from the 2008 recession, the emergence of carbon fiber technology as a consumer standard, and the early-stage adoption of e-bike technology

Dashboard Objective & Scope The dashboard serves as a central intelligence hub for a multinational retail operation. It tracks sales across three core categories—Bikes, Accessories, and Clothing—spanning major global markets (typically United States, Canada, France, Germany, United Kingdom, and Australia). The primary goal is to visualize how macroeconomic shifts and consumer trends between 2011 and 2016 impacted revenue, profit margins, and order volumes.

ategory-Specific Analysis (2011–2016) A. Bikes (The Revenue Engine) From 2011 to 2016, "Bikes" remained the primary revenue driver, often accounting for 80–90% of total sales value. Performance Trends: The 2011–2013 period showed steady growth in Mountain Bikes as geometry standards evolved. By 2014–2016, the "Gravel Bike" category began to emerge as a sub-segment, while high-end Road Bikes saw a surge due to professional cycling's growing popularity. Key Metrics: Total Sales Amount, Unit Price (often $500–$4,000+), and Profit Margin (typically lower than accessories due to manufacturing costs). Observation: Large spikes in bike sales often occurred in Q2 and Q3 (Spring/Summer) in the Northern Hemisphere.

B. Accessories (The Margin Booster) This category includes high-volume items like helmets, tires, tubes, bottles, and fenders. Performance Trends: While the individual price point is low, the volume is massive. This category historically maintained the highest profit margins (often 40%–60%). The "Attachment" Strategy: The dashboard tracks the "Attachment Rate"—how many accessories are sold per bike. A successful country manager in 2013 would look for a high correlation between bike sales and helmet/pump sales. 2015–2016 Shift: There was a notable increase in sales for GPS computers and tech-integrated accessories as riders moved away from simple wired speedometers. Observation: Accessories generated the highest sales but has a lower profit compare to profit generated from bike

C. Clothing (The Retention Driver) Including jerseys, bib shorts, gloves, and shoes, this category bridges the gap between utility and fashion. Performance Trends: This category showed the highest seasonal volatility. Winter gear (jackets, thermal tights) dominated Q4 and Q1, while lightweight kits peaked in Q2. Brand Loyalty: Analysis of this period often shows that clothing sales are a leading indicator of brand loyalty. Customers who buy a bike (one-time purchase) and return for seasonal clothing (recurring purchase) have a higher Lifetime Value (LTV).

Key Visualizations & KPIs A comprehensive dashboard for this period would include: Year-over-Year (YoY) Growth: A line chart comparing 2011 through 2016. Users would likely see a "recovery curve" from 2011–2013 followed by a period of stabilization in 2014–2016. Revenue by Country Map: A geospatial heatmap showing which regions outperformed. For example, Germany often led in "Bikes," while the UK saw surges in "Clothing" during the 2012 Olympic cycling boom. Profit vs. Revenue Column chart: Used to identify that while Bikes bring in the most cash, Accessories bring in the most efficient profit.

Summary The 2011–2016 Country Sales Performance Dashboard is a story of scale and diversification. It illustrates how a business can use high-value Bike sales to acquire customers, while leveraging Accessories and Clothing to sustain high margins and seasonal relevance throughout the year.
